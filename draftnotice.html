UNITED FEDERATION DRAFT NOTICE

From the Desk of the Federal Recruitment Bureau

We regret to inform you... actually, we’re kind of excited!

Hello Citizen,

Congratulations! You’ve been randomly selected (lucky you!) to join the United Federation Armed Forces. You’ve been chosen based on your excellent ability to exist, breathe, and do that thing where you sometimes press buttons on the internet! Your heroic talents are about to be put to good use.

What to Expect:

  • Free Uniform! Ever wanted to wear the same thing every day? Now’s your chance! Stylish, right?
  • Top-Notch Equipment: You’ll be equipped with state-of-the-art A pistol, and a knife. No, we’re not kidding. It is to ensure that you cannot RK.
  • Cardio for Days: Who needs the gym when you’ve got endless running, ducking, and covering to look forward to?
  • Adventure awaits! (Though, full disclosure: it’s mostly a bit of soaking up bullets.)

Your New Responsibilities:

  • Report to Boot Camp within 7 business days. Don’t worry, we won’t start charging late fees until the 8th day.
  • Practice yelling “YES, SIR/MA'AM” at inanimate objects for fun and profit.
  • Make sure your pistol and knife skills are at least on par with video game tutorials (if not, there’s always Google).

What You Get in Return:

  • Instant Respect! (Well, from your fellow recruits. Civilians may just ask why you're wearing all that stuff.)
  • Adventure! Whether it’s securing peace or finding the nearest field latrine, excitement is guaranteed.
  • A Medal (if you don’t mess up too badly).

Don’t worry about those civilian things like “life plans” or “personal ambitions,” the Federation has already figured those out for you. You're welcome! We'll see you on the field, soldier. If you have any questions, remember, your commander prefers those to be asked... after boot camp.

So grab your gear, tighten your laces, and let’s go! (No pressure. But seriously, show up.)

Signed,
The United Federation Draft Office
(Where the real work is done sitting at a desk)

P.S. This isn’t optional. We just like making it sound fun.
