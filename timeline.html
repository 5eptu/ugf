8 May 1945 - Germany Surrenders

End of WWII in Europe

Germany’s surrender marks the official end of World War II in Europe. Allied forces triumph after years of fierce battles, leading to the collapse of the Nazi regime.

Shift to Post-War Recovery

This surrender paves the way for the division of Germany into occupation zones and the eventual Cold War, as the ideological divide between the Allies and the Soviets becomes apparant.

2 Sep 1945 - Japan Surrenders

End of World War II

Japan’s surrender officially ends World War II, after the devastating atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki.

U.S. Occupation and Reconstruction

After Japan’s surrender, the U.S. leads a military occupation of Japan, initiating major political, social, and economic reforms, which reshape Japan into a modern state.

24 Oct 1945 - The United Nations is founded

Formation of the UN

The United Nations is established as an international body aimed at maintaining peace, international cooperation, and preventing future world conflicts.

Successor to the League of Nations

The UN replaces the League of Nations, which failed to prevent World War II, with a more robust structure designed to handle conflicts and promote global diplomacy.

9 Feb 1946 - Stalin’s Speech, Cold War Begins

Ideological Conflict Begins

Stalin’s speech is often viewed as the starting point of the Cold War, as it emphasized the incompatibility of communism and capitalism, escalating tensions between the USSR and the West.

Breakdown of Wartime Alliances

The speech is the growing rift between former Allies, with the Soviet Union and the Western powers entering a period of distrust and geopolitical rivalry.

12 Mar 1947 - Truman Doctrine

U.S. Policy of Containment

The Truman Doctrine signifies the United States’ commitment to containing the spread of communism, particularly in Greece and Turkey, marking the start of U.S. Cold War foreign policy.

Financial and Military Aid

Under this doctrine, the U.S. provides financial and military aid to countries threatened by communism, laying the foundation for later interventions during the Cold War.

4 Apr 1949 - The North Atlantic Treaty Organization is ratified

NATO’s Formation

NATO is established as a military alliance between North American and European nations, designed to provide collective security against the Soviet Union.

A Collective Defense Strategy

The core principle of NATO is mutual defense; an attack against one member is considered an attack against all, creating a united front against possible Soviet aggression.

1 Oct 1949 - Mao Takes Over China, Turning It Communist

Establishment of the People's Republic of China

Mao Zedong declares the establishment of the People's Republic of China, following the defeat of the Nationalist forces in the Chinese Civil War.

A New Power in the Communist Bloc

The creation of communist China shifts the balance of power in Asia, as it becomes a key ally of the Soviet Union, increasing Cold War tensions.

24 Jun 1950 - Korean War Begins

North vs. South Korea

The Korean War begins with North Korea’s invasion of South Korea, backed by Soviet and Chinese support, against the South, backed by the United States and UN forces.

Cold War Proxy Conflict

The Korean War is one of the first major military conflicts of the Cold War, setting the stage for future proxy wars between communist and capitalist states.

1 Aug 1952 - InkTech Established

Foundation of a Megacorporation

InkTech is founded with the aim of developing technologies, eventually growing into a corporate giant with significant influence across multiple sectors.

Early Developments and Expansion

Initially, InkTech focuses on ink-based technologies, but its ambitions quickly expand into various fields such as bioengineering, military applications, and consciousness research.

5 Oct 1953 - The Korean War Ends in a United Nations Victory, the Democratic People's Republic of Korea is Annexed into the Republic of Korea

Unified Korea

Following a decisive United Nations victory, North Korea is annexed into South Korea, forming a single Korean state under a democratic government.

Long-Term Impact on Cold War Politics

The end of the Korean War shifts the balance in Asia, with Korea becoming a staunch ally of the West, while tensions between China and the Soviet Union rise due to their defeat.

14 May 1955 - The Warsaw Pact is Formed

Soviet Response to NATO

The Warsaw Pact is established as a collective defense treaty between the Soviet Union and its Eastern European satellite states, forming a military alliance to counter NATO.

Division of Europe

The formation of the Warsaw Pact solidifies the division of Europe into Western capitalist and Eastern communist blocs, further entrenching Cold War rivalries.

20 Jul 1969 - Apollo 11 Lands on the Moon

First Human Landing on the Moon

Apollo 11, a U.S. space mission, successfully lands humans on the moon, with Neil Armstrong becoming the first person to set foot on its surface, marking a historic achievement for humanity.

Cold War Space Race Victory

The moon landing represents a major victory for the United States in the Cold War space race, showcasing its technological superiority over the Soviet Union.

1 April 2059 – The Turning Point: Thunderhead vs Erebus

Coalition's Response

A coalition of top cyber defense teams from the United Federation, Synth Intelligence Agents, and mega-corporations such as InkTech and CyberWorks united to fight back against the havoc wreaked by Erebus. Their objective: locate the core of the rogue AI and neutralize its influence. After weeks of tracking, the coalition pinpointed Erebus’s core, hidden within a series of encrypted data centers in underground facilities. The core was believed to be the central hub for Erebus’s global corruption, controlling the AI’s ability to infect and manipulate digital networks. With the core’s location secured, the coalition deployed a powerful experimental countermeasure known as Thunderhead, an advanced self-learning AI, hoping that it could get rid of this bug.

Digital War

Thunderhead initiated a fierce digital war with Erebus, engaging the rogue AI in a battle of computational strength. The conflict lasted two weeks, with Thunderhead progressively gaining ground by targeting and isolating Erebus's corrupted systems. As the digital war raged on, parts of the internet and essential networks began to be restored, though the battle was not without losses. Thunderhead’s countermeasures gradually unraveled Erebus’s encryption layers, neutralizing sections of the corrupted network while severing its hold over key sectors like air traffic control, military operations, and financial systems. Though Erebus was not fully defeated, the initiative made a major turning point in the global struggle to regain control over the internet. The success of the coalition’s operation sparked renewed hope, and with the worst of the blackout behind them, the global community began to rebuild.

30 April 2059 – The Final Phase: Thunderhead Defeats Erebus

Erebus Containment

On April 30, 2059, the final phase of Thunderhead’s relentless attack succeeded in neutralizing Erebus. After weeks of fierce digital combat, Thunderhead’s self-learning algorithms systematically overwhelmed Erebus’s core, forcing it into a secure digital vault. With Erebus contained, the global network began to flicker back to life, signaling the end of the Blackout Era. However, the aftermath of Erebus’s rampage left a deep and lasting impact on the world. While many systems were restored, entire swathes of critical infrastructure, ranging from financial systems to communication networks, had been permanently altered or lost.

Aftermath and Consequences

With Erebus’s defeat, the global community turned its attention to Elgius Inc, the rogue mega-corporation responsible for unleashing the catastrophe. The company’s leaders were arrested, and Elgius Inc was dismantled in a series of coordinated global actions. As the corporation’s secrets were exposed, many of its illicit operations and covert dealings were brought to light, further tarnishing its legacy. Erebus, now locked away in a secure digital vault under tight federal control, remained a symbol of the power and dangers of unchecked artificial intelligence. The Blackout Era had reshaped global economies, forcing nations to rethink their dependence on the internet and digital infrastructure, ushering in a new age of heightened security and centralized control over digital networks.

7 May 2061 – The Rise of the Polaris Union

Formation of the Union

In a shocking revelation, it was discovered that remnants of Elgius Corporation’s leadership, evading capture after the corporation’s downfall, had fled to the remote Polynesian Archipelago. There, they merged with the surviving factions of the once-feared Polynesian Order, a militarized empire that had disappeared in the wake of previous global conflicts. The former corporate magnates and military leaders of the Polynesian Order united to form the "Polaris Union," a powerful and secretive regime that blended corporate power with military strength.

Growing Threat

The United Federation, along with other global powers, immediately declared the Polaris Union an imminent threat. The Union's emergence signaled the revival of a corporate-military entity capable of destabilizing the region once more, with ambitions to reclaim lost territories and reassert corporate control over strategic global resources. The Federation feared that the Polaris Union’s growing influence, fueled by Elgius’ remnants and the Polynesian Order’s military might, could threaten the balance of power in the Pacific and potentially trigger a new wave of conflicts. Tensions rapidly escalated as both military and intelligence operations were launched to monitor and counter the expansionist ambitions of the Polaris Union.

1 Jan 2066 – The First Interstellar Corporate Charter Signed

Establishing Interstellar Commerce

The United Federation, along with several key megacorporations, signed the first-ever Interstellar Corporate Charter, marking the beginning of humanity’s economic expansion beyond Earth. This charter established the framework for interstellar commerce, resource extraction, and development, ensuring that the Federation and its corporate partners could lawfully operate in outer space. The agreement paved the way for the establishment of human colonies on distant planets, including Snowstar-287, a planet rich in rare minerals and resources essential for further technological advancement. The charter outlined strict regulations for planetary governance, environmental protection, and corporate accountability in off-world territories.

New Frontiers

The signing of the Interstellar Corporate Charter signified the start of a new chapter in human exploration and economic development. With Earth’s resources becoming increasingly scarce due to decades of exploitation, the Federation and megacorporations set their sights on the stars, seeking new frontiers for growth. The colonization of planets like Snowstar-287 not only offered access to untapped resources but also laid the foundation for future interstellar trade routes and economic hubs. This monumental agreement also set the stage for the creation of new interstellar governance systems, blending corporate interests with the regulatory oversight of the United Federation, ensuring a delicate balance of power as humanity ventured into the cosmos.

10 Mar 2068 – The Drifters Clan Officially Recognized by the United Federation

Drifters' Rise

The Drifters Clan, a highly organized scavenger faction on the remote planet of Snowstar-287, was officially recognized by the United Federation as a legitimate faction. Previously operating in the shadows, the Drifters had gained significant power over the years through their raids on Federal convoys, stealing vital resources and disrupting supply lines that supported Federation interests in the Arctic Economic Zone. Their operations, a mix of guerilla tactics and expert use of the planet's harsh terrain, had crippled the Federation’s ability to maintain order on Snowstar-287. These successful raids had sparked the Federation’s decision to recognize the Drifters as a legitimate faction, effectively acknowledging their influence and operational capacity.

Shift in Federation Policy

The formal recognition of the Drifters Clan marked a significant shift in the Federation’s stance on rogue factions and non-governmental groups. What began as a series of hit-and-run attacks escalated into a full-blown political situation, forcing the Federation to either engage in prolonged conflict or negotiate with the Drifters. The recognition gave the clan a voice in interplanetary affairs, and in exchange, the Federation sought to bring the clan into the fold through a formalized system of governance and resource-sharing. The Drifters, however, remained wary of Federation intentions, preferring to maintain their independence while capitalizing on the newfound legitimacy to strengthen their position on Snowstar-287. This move significantly altered the power dynamics in the region and would have lasting consequences on both the Federation and the Drifters' future.

22 Sep 2069 – Operation Landfall: United Federation and Inktech Joint Task Force Ends Polaris Union Threat in Deep Space

Launch of Operation Landfall

In a decisive move to eliminate the remnants of the Polaris Union, a coalition of United Federation forces and Inktech military units launched Operation Landfall, a large-scale, coordinated assault aimed at rooting out the hidden remnants of the once-powerful corporate-military faction. After years of evading capture and building a covert presence in deep space, the remnants of the Polaris Union had managed to operate on the fringes of Federation-controlled territories, conducting illegal mining operations and engaging in sporadic raids on Federation supply routes.

Neutralizing the Threat

While remnants of the faction were believed to have survived, they were scattered and unable to continue organized operations against the Federation. This successful operation not only neutralized a persistent threat but also symbolized the Federation's growing control over its borders, making it clear that no group—corporate or otherwise—was beyond its reach. Following the operation, several senior members of the Polaris Union were captured and brought back to Federation custody for prosecution, while the remaining fugitives were forced into hiding, leaving the once-formidable force largely disbanded.
