United Federation Medical Corps

1. Rules of Tryout

To maintain the integrity and professionalism of the United Federation Medical Corps, all recruits must adhere to the following rules during the tryout process:

  • Respect: Treat all individuals with respect and dignity, including fellow recruits and trainers.
  • Punctuality: Arrive on time for all training sessions to demonstrate commitment and responsibility.
  • Focus: Remain attentive during training exercises; distractions can jeopardize safety and learning.
  • Communication: Use clear and respectful language at all times; effective communication is essential in medical situations.
  • Commitment: Show a strong dedication to the values and mission of the Medical Corps; your determination is crucial.

Script for Officers:

Welcome recruits! Today marks the beginning of your journey with the United Federation Medical Corps. Remember, respect and punctuality are crucial. Pay close attention to the instructions given and stay focused. Let's work together to ensure everyone adheres to these rules. Good luck!

2. Role of Medical Corps

The United Federation Medical Corps plays a vital role in ensuring operational readiness and effectiveness. The primary responsibilities of the Corps include:

  • Providing Medical Care: Deliver immediate medical assistance to injured soldiers on the battlefield.
  • Training and Education: Equip recruits and officers with essential medical procedures and emergency response tactics.
  • Coordination: Work closely with other military units to ensure seamless medical support in diverse operational environments.
  • Emergency Response: Be prepared to respond quickly and effectively to medical emergencies, ensuring personnel safety.

Script for Officers:

As part of the United Federation Medical Corps, your primary role is to provide medical care and support during missions. You will train extensively to ensure you are prepared for any medical emergencies. Your actions can mean the difference between life and death. Let's get started!

3. Basic Awareness Test (Adhering to Face Commands)

To demonstrate understanding of face commands and situational awareness, recruits must complete the Basic Awareness Test, which includes:

  • Attention: Stand still and focus on the person giving commands.
  • Forward March: Move in a straight line to the designated area when instructed.
  • Halt: Stop immediately and wait for further directions.
  • Medical Attention: Quickly identify the nearest injured personnel and assess their condition.

Script for Officers:

Today, we will conduct the Basic Awareness Test. It's crucial that you pay attention to my commands and respond appropriately. This exercise is designed to enhance your readiness in the field. Let's begin the test!

4. Communication and Teamwork

Effective communication and teamwork are vital skills for all medical officers. Recruits will participate in exercises aimed at improving these abilities, including:

  • Role-Playing Scenarios: Engage in simulated emergency situations to practice communication under pressure.
  • Debriefing Sessions: Discuss experiences after exercises to learn from one another and improve performance.
  • Signal and Command Drills: Communicate using verbal commands and non-verbal signals during chaotic situations.

5. Locating Injured Personnel and Extraction

Recruits will learn crucial procedures for locating and safely extracting injured personnel from hazardous environments. Key points include:

  • Assessment: Quickly assess the scene for any dangers before approaching injured personnel.
  • Extraction Techniques: Practice safe methods to move injured individuals without causing further harm.
  • Reporting: Communicate findings with command and request any necessary backup or additional support once stabilized.

Script for Officers:

In the field, your ability to locate and safely extract injured personnel can save lives. Today, we will practice the assessment and extraction techniques necessary to handle such situations. Remember to report all findings to command. Let's begin the practical exercises!
