United Federation Medical Corps

1. Rules of Tryout

The following rules must be followed by all recruits during the tryout process to maintain the integrity of the United Federation Medical Corps:

  • Respect: All recruits must treat each other, as well as trainers and officers, with utmost respect.
  • Punctuality: Arrive on time for all scheduled training sessions. Late arrivals may be disqualified.
  • Focus: Remain attentive and engaged during all training exercises. Distractions can lead to serious consequences in the field.
  • Communication: Use clear and respectful language at all times. Effective communication is critical in medical situations.
  • Commitment: Show a strong commitment to the values and mission of the Medical Corps. Your determination is essential.

Script for Officers:

Welcome recruits! Today marks the beginning of your journey with the United Federation Medical Corps. Remember, respect and punctuality are crucial. Pay close attention to the instructions given and stay focused. Let's work together to ensure everyone adheres to these rules. Good luck!

2. Role of Medical Corps

The United Federation Medical Corps is integral to the operational readiness and effectiveness of our forces. The primary responsibilities of the Medical Corps include:

  • Providing Medical Care: Medical Corps personnel are responsible for providing immediate medical assistance to injured soldiers on the battlefield.
  • Training and Education: The Corps trains recruits and officers on essential medical procedures and emergency response tactics.
  • Coordination: Medical Corps members must work closely with other military units to ensure seamless medical support in diverse operational environments.
  • Emergency Response: In the event of medical emergencies, the Corps must be ready to respond quickly and effectively, ensuring the safety of all personnel.

Script for Officers:

As part of the United Federation Medical Corps, your primary role is to provide medical care and support during missions. You will train extensively to ensure you are prepared for any medical emergencies. Your actions can mean the difference between life and death. Let's get started!

3. Basic Awareness Test (Adhering to Face Commands)

Recruits must complete the Basic Awareness Test to demonstrate their understanding of face commands and situational awareness. This test includes:

  • Attention: Stand still and focus on the person giving commands.
  • Forward March: Move in a straight line to the designated area when instructed.
  • Halt: Stop immediately and wait for further directions.
  • Medical Attention: Quickly identify the nearest injured personnel and assess their condition.

Script for Officers:

Today, we will conduct the Basic Awareness Test. It's crucial that you pay attention to my commands and respond appropriately. This exercise is designed to enhance your readiness in the field. Let's begin the test!

4. Communication and Teamwork

Effective communication and teamwork are vital skills for all medical officers. Recruits will participate in exercises aimed at improving these abilities, including:

  • Role-Playing Scenarios: Engage in simulated emergency situations to practice communication under pressure.
  • Debriefing Sessions: After exercises, recruits will discuss their experiences to learn from one another and improve future performance.
  • Signal and Command Drills: Learn to communicate using verbal commands and non-verbal signals during chaotic situations.

5. Locating Injured Personnel and Extraction

Recruits will learn the crucial procedures for locating and safely extracting injured personnel from hazardous environments. Key points include:

  • Assessment: Quickly assess the scene for any dangers before approaching injured personnel.
  • Extraction Techniques: Practice safe methods to move injured individuals without causing further harm.
  • Reporting: Once the situation is stabilized, communicate with command and request any necessary backup or additional support.

Script for Officers:

In the field, your ability to locate and safely extract injured personnel can save lives. Today, we will practice the assessment and extraction techniques necessary to handle such situations. Remember to report all findings to command. Let's begin the practical exercises!
