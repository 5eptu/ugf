1. Rules of Tryout

To maintain the integrity and effectiveness of the United Federation Medical Corps, all recruits must adhere to the following rules during the tryout process:

  • Respect: Treat all personnel with respect, including fellow recruits and trainers.
  • Punctuality: Arrive on time for all scheduled training sessions and evaluations.
  • Focus: Pay attention to instructions and engage actively in all training exercises.
  • Communication: Use clear and respectful language when communicating with officers and peers.
  • Commitment: Show dedication to the values and mission of the Medical Corps.

Script for Officers:

Welcome recruits! You are here today to begin your journey with the United Federation Medical Corps. Remember, respect and punctuality are crucial. As we progress through the training, stay focused and committed. Let’s ensure we all adhere to these rules. Good luck!

2. Role of Medical Corps

The United Federation Medical Corps plays a vital role in the operational readiness and safety of our forces. The key responsibilities include:

  • Providing Medical Care: Ensure immediate medical assistance to injured personnel during missions.
  • Training and Education: Equip officers and recruits with the necessary skills and knowledge for medical emergencies.
  • Coordination: Work closely with other units to facilitate medical support in various operational contexts.
  • Emergency Response: Prepare for and respond to medical emergencies, ensuring rapid and effective care.

Script for Officers:

As members of the United Federation Medical Corps, your primary role is to provide medical care and support during missions. This includes immediate assistance, training fellow recruits, and ensuring effective coordination with other units. Remember, your actions can save lives!

3. Basic Awareness Test (Adhering to Face Commands)

All recruits must complete a Basic Awareness Test to demonstrate their ability to follow commands and maintain situational awareness. The test consists of recognizing and responding to face commands, which include:

  • Attention: Stand still and focus on the speaker.
  • Forward March: Move in a straight line toward the designated area.
  • Halt: Stop immediately and await further instructions.
  • Medical Attention: Identify the nearest injured personnel and respond appropriately.

Script for Officers:

Today, we will conduct the Basic Awareness Test. Pay close attention to my face commands, as they will help ensure your readiness in the field. Remember to stay alert and responsive to instructions. Let's begin!

4. Communication and Teamwork

Effective communication and teamwork are essential skills for all medical officers. Recruits will engage in exercises designed to improve their ability to work together under pressure, including:

  • Role-Playing Scenarios: Practice communication in simulated emergency situations.
  • Debriefing Sessions: Discuss experiences and feedback to enhance teamwork.
  • Signal and Command Drills: Learn to communicate using both verbal commands and non-verbal signals.

Script for Officers:

Communication and teamwork are crucial in our operations. Throughout this training, we will practice various scenarios that require clear communication and collaboration. Pay attention to your teammates and work together to ensure effective responses. Let's proceed with the drills!

5. Locating Injured Personnel and Extraction

Recruits will learn the procedures for locating and safely extracting injured personnel. Key points include:

  • Assessment: Quickly assess the scene for hazards and the condition of injured personnel.
  • Extraction Techniques: Utilize proper techniques to safely move injured individuals.
  • Reporting: Communicate the situation to command and request additional support if needed.

Script for Officers:

In the field, your ability to locate and extract injured personnel can make a significant difference. We will practice assessment techniques and extraction methods to ensure you are prepared for real-life scenarios. Always remember to report your findings to command. Let’s begin the practical exercises!
